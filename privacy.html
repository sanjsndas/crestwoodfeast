    • Privacy Policy

    Your data, your kitchen — we treat both with care. This policy explains how Crestwood Feast Journal handles your information.
    📅 Last updated: June 25, 2026  ·  📌 Effective: July 1, 2026

    Our commitment

    At Crestwood Feast Journal, we believe that privacy is a fundamental ingredient in a healthy relationship with our readers. We collect only the information that helps us serve you better — and we never sell or trade your personal data.

    🔒 In short: We collect minimal data, use it only to improve your experience, and protect it like a sourdough starter.

    What we collect

    We may collect the following types of information:

    • Contact form data: name, email address, and any message you send us.
    • Newsletter sign-up: email address (if you opt in).
    • Cookies & analytics: anonymized usage data to understand which recipes are most popular. We use Cookies Policy for details.
    • Comments (if enabled): name, email, and IP address (to prevent spam).

    How we use your information

    • Respond to your inquiries and messages.
    • Send you occasional newsletters (only if you subscribed).
    • Improve our website content and recipe recommendations.
    • Prevent spam and maintain site security.

    Data sharing

    We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We may share limited data with trusted service providers (e.g., email delivery platforms) only to the extent necessary to operate our website. These partners are contractually obligated to keep your data confidential.

    Your rights

    You have the right to:

    • Access the personal data we hold about you.
    • Request correction or deletion of your data.
    • Withdraw consent for marketing communications at any time.
    • Lodge a complaint with a data protection authority.

    To exercise these rights, please contact us.

    Data security

    We use industry-standard measures to protect your data. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. We strive to protect your information but cannot guarantee absolute security.

    Children's privacy

    Our website is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such data, please contact us and we will delete it.

    Changes to this policy

    We may update this policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by posting a notice on this page or via email (if you have subscribed). The "Last updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the policy was last revised.
