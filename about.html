About the journal

Founded in 2016, Crestwood Feast Journal is a quiet corner of the internet dedicated to the craft of cooking — where recipes meet science, and technique meets intuition.

We believe great cooking is built on curiosity. Every recipe we share has been tested, tasted, and refined. Our mission is to equip you with the knowledge to cook with confidence, whether you're a seasoned chef or a curious beginner.

Our story

Crestwood Feast Journal began as a personal notebook — a place to document kitchen experiments, failed loaves, and accidental triumphs. Over the years, it grew into a community of cooks who value precision, flavour, and the joy of discovery.

We don't chase trends. We chase understanding. From the Maillard reaction to the ideal sourdough hydration, we break down the why behind the how — so you can adapt, improvise, and make every recipe your own.

Our editorial standards

  • Rigorous testing: Every recipe is tested in a home kitchen environment, with adjustments for common pitfalls.
  • Clear instructions: We avoid jargon and explain every step — from mise en place to plating.
  • Ingredient integrity: We recommend specific brands and types where they matter, and offer substitutes when they don't.
  • No paid endorsements: Our recommendations are based on quality, not sponsorship.
  • Continuous improvement: We update our guides as we learn more — cooking is a living practice.

Frequently asked questions

Who writes the content?

Crestwood Feast Journal is written by a small team of passionate cooks, led by our founder who has over a decade of professional and home kitchen experience. We collaborate with guest experts for specialist topics.

Can I submit a recipe or suggestion?

Absolutely. We love hearing from our community. Visit our Contact page to share your ideas, ask questions, or suggest topics you'd like us to cover.

How can I support Crestwood Feast Journal?

Sharing our articles, subscribing to our newsletter, and cooking with us is the best support. We also welcome thoughtful feedback — it helps us grow and improve.
