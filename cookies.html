    • 🍪 Cookies Policy

    We use cookies to make your feast better — just like a pinch of salt. Learn what we use and how you can manage them.
    📅 Last updated: June 25, 2026  ·  📌 Effective: July 1, 2026

    What are cookies?

    Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They help us remember your preferences, understand how you interact with our content, and improve your overall experience. Think of them as the kitchen helpers that make everything run smoothly.

    🥄 In short: Cookies help us remember your preferences, analyze site traffic, and serve you better. We never use them to track you across the web for advertising purposes.

    How we use cookies

    We use cookies for the following purposes:

    • Essential cookies: These are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic features like page navigation and access to secure areas.
    • Performance & analytics: We use anonymized data to understand which recipes and articles are most popular. This helps us create more of what you love.
    • Preference cookies: These remember your settings (like language or display preferences) so you don't have to set them each time.
    • Comment cookies: If you leave a comment, we may store your name and email in a cookie for your convenience (so you don't have to re-enter them).

    Types of cookies we use

    • Session cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser.
    • Persistent cookies: These remain on your device for a set period (up to 12 months) or until you delete them.
    • First-party cookies: Set by us directly.
    • Third-party cookies: We use minimal third-party cookies (e.g., for analytics) from trusted partners like Fathom or Plausible — both are privacy-first and do not track you across sites.

    Your choices

    You are in control. You can:

    • Accept or decline cookies via your browser settings.
    • Delete existing cookies at any time.
    • Use your browser's "private" or "incognito" mode to browse without storing cookies.

    Most browsers allow you to manage cookie preferences. You can usually find these settings in the "Options" or "Preferences" menu. For more detailed guidance, visit AboutCookies.org.

    Managing cookies in your browser

    • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data.
    • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data.
    • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data.
    • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions.

    Updates to this policy

    We may update this Cookies Policy occasionally to reflect changes in technology or regulations. We will notify you of significant changes via a notice on our website or by email (if you have subscribed). The "Last updated" date at the top indicates the latest revision.
